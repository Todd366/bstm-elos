# Archetype: Liquidity Type

**Definition:** Demand already exists, but growth is restricted by capital flow, inventory funding, or resource availability — not by visibility.

**Evidence:**
- Trial 006 — Jikisa (3–5 day stockouts on high-demand items despite strong word-of-mouth demand)

**Diagnostic signature:** Existing customer base, active word-of-mouth, functioning POS/terminal data — but terminal logs show repeated stockouts on fast-moving items correlated with capital replenishment cycles, not demand cycles.

**Critical warning:** This archetype is easy to misdiagnose as Exposure Type, because both present as "low revenue." The differentiator is terminal/inventory data, not customer sentiment. Increasing demand on a Liquidity-Type business worsens the outcome — see `BSTM-PRIN-008`.
