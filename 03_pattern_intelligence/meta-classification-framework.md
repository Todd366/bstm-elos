# Meta-Classification Framework
**Status:** 🟡 Experimental — emerging after Trial 006, needs 10+ more trials to confirm

## The Shift

After 6 trials, ELOS is no longer only discovering "business problems." It is beginning to separate businesses into **needs categories** that sit one level above the four archetypes — a classification of classifications.

| Trial | Business Type | Archetype | Main Bottleneck |
|---|---|---|---|
| 001 | Jewelry (Retail) | Exposure | Visibility |
| 002 | Hawkers Beauty Spa (Services) | Exposure | Growth + seasonal competition |
| 003 | Nado Clothes (Mobile Retail) | Agility | Systems + reach |
| 004 | 20-Year Tuckshop (Established Retail) | Multi-System | Modernization resistance |
| 005 | NDS (Multi-business) | Multi-System | Management systems |
| 006 | Jikisa (Multi-business Retail) | Liquidity | Stock capital + inventory |

## The Four Meta-Categories (proposed)

1. **Businesses needing customers** — the Exposure archetype maps here directly. Fix: reach and discoverability.
2. **Businesses needing systems** — the Agility and Multi-System archetypes map here. Fix: coordination, synchronization, per-line accounting.
3. **Businesses needing capital efficiency** — the Liquidity archetype maps here. Fix: restocking velocity, supplier terms, cash-flow timing — never demand generation.
4. **Businesses needing digital transformation** — cross-cutting; present in Trials 003, 004, 005, 006 to varying degrees. Fix: the *right* technology at the *right* adoption pace (see Trial 004's Inconclusive result — technology must adapt to habits).

## Why this matters more than the archetype layer alone

The archetype layer (Layer 3) answers *"what pattern does this trial match?"* The meta-classification layer answers a prior, more strategic question: *"what kind of intervention should even be considered before we look at the pattern?"* Misapplying an Exposure-Type fix (marketing) to a Liquidity-Type business (Trial 006) is exactly the failure mode this layer is designed to prevent.

## Promotion Rule (draft)

A meta-category graduates from 🟡 Experimental to 🟢 Active when:
- At least 10 trials have been classified against it, **and**
- At least 2 documented cases exist where misapplying a *different* meta-category's fix would have measurably worsened the outcome (as in Trial 006).

See `documentation/TRIAL-TO-ARCHETYPE-PROMOTION-RULE.md` for the operational promotion mechanics that will eventually apply to this layer too.

## Open Question (logged for future trials)
By Trial 100, this classification could become one of BSTM's most valuable outputs — not the trials themselves, but the ability to instantly route a new business into the correct meta-category before wasting resources on the wrong fix.
