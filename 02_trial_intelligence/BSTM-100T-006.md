## Context
A single building containing three connected commercial businesses: a liquor store, a butcher, and a general store. The manager operates a modern till/POS system (referred to on-site as "ROS" — exact system name unconfirmed, flagged for follow-up verification).

## Assumption Registry
- **Assumption:** Scaling regional discoverability is the primary path to expanding enterprise revenue.
- Initial Confidence: 4/5

## Hypothesis
Deploying hyper-localized digital outreach will directly expand store cash flows.

## Experiment
Analyzed POS terminal logs and interviewed the on-site manager about operational bottlenecks.

## Raw Evidence
The node has consistent local demand, strong word-of-mouth visibility, and an established customer base. Terminal logs show high-demand stock positions sitting empty for 3–5 days — not due to lack of customers, but due to lack of immediate capital to replenish stock.

## Interpretation
**Correction on record:** This is not a visibility problem. Jikisa has customers, demand, and word-of-mouth advertising already working. The actual ceiling is a **capital velocity bottleneck** — the business cannot restock fast enough to meet the demand it already has. Increasing customer demand on a node that cannot restock accelerates collapse and breaks community trust.

## Confidence Update
- Initial: 4/5
- Final: 1/5
- Reason for change: Terminal verification proved capital constraints, not customer exposure, are the operational ceiling.

## Error Digestion
1. **The Fracture:** The initial strategy focused on visibility solutions, which would have worsened the internal capital-depletion loop.
2. **Why was it unexpected:** Traditional assumptions treat low revenue as a low-traffic problem by default.
3. **Which assumption failed:** The baseline view that exposure is the default limiting factor for local businesses.
4. **What rule changes:** Formulated `BSTM-PRIN-008` (The Visibility Illusion) — see `04_organizational_principles/active_principles/`. Never increase demand when supply velocity is the bottleneck.
