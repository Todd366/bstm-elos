## Assumption Registry
- **Assumption:** Increasing storefront discovery will directly scale sales velocity.
- Initial Confidence: 4/5

## Hypothesis
If visibility (signage, digital location-tagging, marketplace listing) is fixed, existing foot traffic converts to sales without needing to acquire new customers.

## Experiment
Deployed a baseline structured digital catalog instance on the BSTM Marketplace and logged current storefront visibility as a control condition.

## Raw Evidence
Zero external signage identifiable from outside the shop despite being embedded in a high-traffic bus rank. Only 2–3 customers observed entering during the interview window despite continuous foot traffic passing the general area. Post-listing (from prior trial cycle data): 45 unique profile views recorded within 48 hours once a digital catalog was live.

## Interpretation
The business has quality inventory and steady adjacent foot traffic; its primary ceiling is an exposure bottleneck — specifically physical *and* digital invisibility — not product utility, price, or staffing.

## Confidence Update
- Initial: 4/5
- Final: 5/5
- Reason for change: Field checklist independently corroborated the exposure hypothesis (Poor rating on Marketing & Branding, Poor on Visible Weaknesses) before any intervention was applied — strong convergent evidence.
